I hold a Ph.D. in Translational Medical and Surgical Science from the University of Parma, where I conduct research at the intersection of aerobiology, medical entomology, and public health epidemiology. My work focuses on how biological and environmental factors drive human health outcomes — from airborne pollen exposures to arthropod-borne disease dynamics.

My aerobiology research centres on the monitoring and classification of airborne biological particles — pollen grains and fungal spores — using automated devices including the Poleno-99 in Parma. This data informs both allergy forecasting and broader climate–health relationships in the Po Valley.

In parallel, my entomological work examines the ecology and medical significance of arthropod vectors relevant to WHO-priority diseases. I apply a One Health framework to connect insect surveillance, environmental change, and population-level disease risk — translating field observations into actionable public health intelligence.

I collaborate closely with Prof. Roberto Albertini and Prof. Cesira Pasquarella at the University of Parma. My research is published in Medline-indexed journals, reflecting my commitment to rigorous, evidence-based science.

Active areas

Research focus

Aerobiology

Monitoring and classifying airborne biological particles — pollen grains and fungal spores — using volumetric samplers and automated devices including the Poleno-99 in Parma. Data pipelines are built in Python and R to support allergy forecasting and environmental health research.

Medical Entomology

Investigating the ecology, distribution, and public health significance of arthropod vectors — including mosquitoes and ticks — with relevance to WHO-priority vector-borne diseases across European and Mediterranean contexts.

Vector-borne Disease Surveillance

Designing and evaluating integrated surveillance systems for vector-borne diseases. Combines entomological field data, epidemiological modelling, and geographic information systems (GIS) to track transmission risk in real time.

Environmental & Public Health

Examining the links between environmental exposures, climate change, and human health outcomes. Applies a One Health framework to bridge ecosystem dynamics, animal health, and human epidemiology in policy-relevant research.
